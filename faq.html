Questions, answered honestly.

What kinds of dogs do you work with?

Sensitive, shy, anxious, fearful, reactive, or misunderstood dogs of all sizes. Maybe other walkers have been scared of your dog, unable to connect, or you’ve had bad experiences before. If so, I’m your gal.

What does “force-free” and “nervous-system-attuned” actually mean?

Force-free means I never use pain, fear, or intimidation — no prong or shock collars, no leash corrections, no dragging a scared dog past something terrifying. Nervous-system-attuned means I read your dog’s stress signals in real time and adjust the walk — route, pace, distance from triggers — so they stay in the zone where they feel safe enough to learn and enjoy themselves.

What’s the meet-and-greet like?

Low-key. I come to you, meet your dog on their terms, and we see how everyone feels. Some dogs take the whole 30 minutes just deciding whether to sniff me. That’s completely fine. No commitment required.

What if my dog has never warmed up to a new walker?

Totally okay. We go as slowly as they need. Trust builds at their pace — not mine, not yours. Some of my favorite client relationships started with a dog hiding behind the couch.

Why don’t you offer one-off walks?

Because they don’t work for sensitive dogs. A fearful dog meeting a new person once gains nothing — and may lose ground. Weekly rhythm with the same trusted person is how these dogs actually make progress. It’s the difference between an interruption in their day and a relationship in their life.

My dog is reactive to other dogs. Can you still walk them?

Very likely yes — dog-reactive dogs are a big part of my practice. All walks are solo (never group), and I choose routes and times that keep your dog under threshold, gradually building their confidence at a distance they can handle.

Which neighborhoods do you cover?

Maple Leaf, Roosevelt, Wedgwood, Ravenna, Fremont, and Wallingford in North Seattle. On the edge of one of those? Ask — I’ll tell you honestly whether I can make the schedule work.

Are you licensed, bonded, and insured?

Not yet — and I’d rather tell you that plainly than bury it. I’m an owner-operated business working toward my certifications (force-free training and pet first aid) with licensing and insurance in progress. What I can offer today: 10+ years of experience, references on request, and total transparency about how I work. This answer will be updated the day each box is checked.

What happens in bad weather?

Seattle drizzle? We walk (many dogs love it). Genuinely unsafe conditions — ice, extreme heat, smoke — and I’ll swap the walk for indoor enrichment and decompression at your home, or reschedule. Your dog’s safety always wins.

How do you get into my home?

Most clients use a lockbox or door code; I treat keys and codes with bank-level care and confirm every entry/exit in your walk note.

What’s your cancellation policy?

Life happens. Give me 24 hours’ notice and there’s no charge; same-day cancellations are billed at half rate. If I ever have to cancel, that walk is free to reschedule and I’ll give you as much notice as humanly possible.

Still wondering if we’re a fit?

There’s no wrong question, and no judgment here. The easiest way to find out is a low-key, no-commitment meet-and-greet.
