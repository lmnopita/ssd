Hi, I’m Lily.

I’m a force-free dog walker in North Seattle and the “difficult” dogs are my favorites: the shy, the barky, the overwhelmed.

My story

I’ve been walking dogs for over ten years and somewhere along the way the “difficult” dogs became my favorites: the shy ones, the barkers, the dogs who hide behind their humans, who’ve been dropped by other walkers, who need twenty minutes of sniffing before they can say hello.

I started Seattle Sensitive Dogs because these dogs deserve more than someone willing to tolerate them. They deserve someone who genuinely delights in them and has the knowledge to help them feel safe.

That knowledge has real roots. It began with my own dog, an AKC Canine Good Citizen pit bull, and with the Brew City Bully Club in Milwaukee (now Pitbull Advocates of America), a nonprofit doing advocacy and owner-handler training. From there I trained in all-breed reactive dog work through the Dog Den’s “Scrappers” program in Madison and volunteered at the Dane County Humane Society. In Seattle, my practice has grown around fearful and sensitive dogs, so I’m at home with either side of the fearful coin: the submissive and the reactive alike.

My philosophy

Calm guidance. Real connection.

My approach is force-free and grounded in how dogs’ nervous systems actually work. Every dog has a window of tolerance, the zone where they can take in the world, learn, and enjoy themselves. Push past it and you get lunging, freezing, shutdown. My job is to keep walks inside that window and to help widen it over time.

That means: I watch your dog’s body language continuously. I adjust distance, pace, and route in real time. I never punish fear. And I treat every walk as one more deposit in the trust bank.

Why I keep my roster small

I only work with a small, weekly roster, because consistency is the whole point. Sensitive dogs build trust through rhythm with the same person; one-off visits don’t get them there. So I keep my list short and take on dog parents who want to build their dog a calmer, fuller life.
