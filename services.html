Why weekly-only is the method

I only take weekly clients, because that’s what actually works. Sensitive dogs don’t build trust in one-off visits; they build it through rhythm and repetition, with the same person at the same times each week. It asks something of you, too: real progress needs a dog parent who’s genuinely in it with me.

A fearful dog meeting a new walker once gains nothing and can lose ground. Weekly rhythm is how these dogs make progress: the difference between an interruption in their day and a relationship in their life.

Weekly walks

Solo walks, paced to your dog: typically 30–45 minutes door to door. We’ll build the rhythm that fits your dog and your week.

What every walk includes

Clarity builds trust.

  • A solo walk, one-on-one, never a group or pack
  • A behavioral note with photos and a map of our route
  • Regulation-focused pacing that keeps your dog inside their window of tolerance
  • Loose-leash and confidence work woven in
  • The same trusted walker, me, Lily, every single time

What I don’t do

  • Group walks or pack walks
  • One-off or occasional walks
  • Aversive tools or corrections (prong, e-collar, leash pops, flooding)
  • Handing your dog to a substitute walker
